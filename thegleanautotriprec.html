A breathtaking natural wonder, Niagara Falls is known for its massive, thunderous waterfalls and stunning views. It’s a must-see destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.
Located in downtown Toronto, the CF Toronto Eaton Centre is a vibrant shopping mall featuring hundreds of stores, restaurants, and architectural highlights like its famous glass ceiling.
Ontario’s premier four-season resort, Blue Mountain offers skiing, snowboarding, mountain biking, hiking, and a charming pedestrian village with shops and cafes.
One of Canada’s most iconic landmarks, the CN Tower soars above Toronto’s skyline and offers sweeping views, a glass floor, and the thrilling EdgeWalk experience.
Canada’s largest museum of art, culture, and nature, the ROM features world-renowned exhibits from ancient artifacts to dinosaur skeletons.
This underwater attraction, located near the CN Tower, showcases thousands of marine animals, from colorful tropical fish to giant sharks, with its famous glass tunnel walk.
A popular summer destination in Toronto, Woodbine Beach offers sandy shores, volleyball courts, picnic areas, and a lively boardwalk by Lake Ontario.
Home to over 90,000 works of art, the AGO is one of the largest art museums in North America, featuring Canadian, Indigenous, and international pieces.
One of the largest zoos in the world, the Toronto Zoo houses over 5,000 animals across diverse regions like the African Savanna and Tundra Trek.
Canada’s largest amusement park, located just north of Toronto, offers thrilling roller coasters, water rides, live shows, and a giant waterpark.
A sprawling protected area inside Toronto, Rouge Park offers hiking trails, wetlands, forests, and historic sites, making it a rare natural oasis within a busy city.
